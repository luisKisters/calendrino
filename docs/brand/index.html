01 · Logo concepts

Ten directions for the mark

All fuse two ideas — a calendar (the destination) and capture / AI spark (the input). Each is a real SVG on the squircle app tile, readable down to 48px. #1 is my recommended flagship; it’s also saved as a standalone file at docs/brand/calendrino-mark.svg.

01

Spark Cell ★

A clean calendar where one day glows as an AI sparkle — “the magic day.” Calm, instantly legible.

Flagship · app icon + favicon
02

Aperture Date

A camera lens with a tiny calendar at its focal point. Reads strongly as “capture,” with a glint of light.

Best when “scan / camera” leads
03

Line Badge

Ultra-minimal white line-art calendar + spark on solid indigo. Lots of negative space, very modern.

Best for monochrome / small sizes
04

“C” Spark Monogram

The letter C drawn as an open camera ring, punctuated by the AI spark in its mouth. Great as a solo glyph.

Best for avatar / app-store tile
05

Focus Frame

Camera focus brackets locking onto a date. Says “point, capture, it becomes an event.”

Best for onboarding / camera screen
06

In a Flash

A bolt cutting through the calendar — speed and instant capture. Energetic, action-forward.

Best for marketing / motion
07

Time Lens

A viewfinder whose reticle is a calendar grid — “focus on a moment in time.” Techy and precise.

Best for a developer-tool tone
08

When & Where

A map-pin shaped from a calendar — fuses time + place, the two things an event needs. Friendly silhouette.

Best if location features grow
09

Any → Event

A photo/PDF behind a calendar — “whatever you throw at it becomes a calendar entry.” Tells the story.

Best for explainer / hero art
10

Done & Dusted

Calendar + confirming check + spark — emphasizes the satisfying “added!” payoff of the flow.

Best for success states / favicon-alt

06 · AI image prompts

Copy-paste prompts for ChatGPT / Gemini / Midjourney

A master prompt for the flagship, per-concept variants, and a negative prompt. Hit copy and paste into your image model of choice. For Midjourney append --ar 1:1 --style raw.

Master prompt · flagship “Spark Cell”
App icon for "Calendrino", an app that turns a photo or document into a calendar event. A clean, modern, minimalist icon: a single bold white calendar page with a small top binding, where one date cell is replaced by a glowing 4-point AI sparkle. Flat vector with soft depth (subtle inner highlight, gentle long shadow). Background: smooth diagonal gradient from indigo #6366F1 to violet #8B5CF6 on a rounded-square (squircle) tile. Foreground in crisp white with a light-indigo accent (#C7D2FE). Geometric, balanced, high contrast, instantly readable at 48px, generous safe-area padding. No text, no letters, no numbers, no photographic elements. 1024×1024, centered, app-store quality, sharp edges.
02 · Aperture Date
Minimalist app icon: a white camera lens / aperture ring with a tiny calendar grid at its focal centre and a single bright glint of light. Indigo→violet gradient squircle (#6366F1→#8B5CF6), white glyph, flat vector with soft depth. No text/numbers. 1024×1024, centered, app-store quality.
03 · Line Badge
Ultra-clean app icon: minimal white line-art calendar with a small spark, lots of negative space, on a solid deep-indigo squircle (#4F46E5). Thin-but-safe strokes, geometric, balanced. No text/numbers/photos. 1024×1024, centered.
06 · In a Flash
App icon: a white calendar page with a lightning bolt cutting through it, suggesting instant capture. Indigo→violet gradient squircle (#6366F1→#8B5CF6), flat vector, soft depth, high contrast. No text/numbers. 1024×1024, centered, app-store quality.
09 · Any → Event
App icon: a photo / document card fanned behind a white calendar with a small AI sparkle, showing "any input becomes an event". Indigo→violet gradient squircle (#6366F1→#8B5CF6), flat vector with soft depth. No text/numbers. 1024×1024, centered.
Negative prompt · paste into the “avoid” field
no text, no words, no letters, no numbers, no realistic photos, not busy, no clutter, no thin strokes that disappear when small, no hard drop shadow on a transparent background, no gradients on the glyph itself, no skeuomorphic leather/paper texture
07 · Design tokens

Drop-in CSS variables

The recommended palette as ready-to-paste tokens for src/index.css (Tailwind v4 @theme or plain custom properties). Matches the values used throughout this page.

tokens.css · Night Indigo · Dream Violet
:root{
  /* surfaces */
  --bg:            #0B0F1A;
  --bg-deep:       #050608;
  --surface:       #141A2B;
  --surface-2:     #192134;
  --border:        rgba(255,255,255,.08);
  --border-strong: rgba(255,255,255,.14);

  /* brand */
  --primary:       #6366F1;   /* indigo  */
  --primary-deep:  #4338CA;
  --violet:        #8B5CF6;
  --indigo-text:   #A5B4FC;   /* indigo that passes as text on dark */
  --grad:          linear-gradient(135deg,#6366F1,#8B5CF6);

  /* text */
  --text:          #E8EAF2;   /* ~13:1 on --bg  */
  --muted:         #94A3B8;   /* ~4.8:1 on --bg */
  --faint:         #5B657D;

  /* status */
  --success:       #10B981;
  --danger:        #F87171;

  /* shape + motion */
  --radius:        16px;
  --radius-sm:     12px;
  --easing:        cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1);
}
Quick wins for the real app: swap the emoji icons (📅 📷 ⚙️) for a single SVG icon set (Lucide), add a glow shadow behind the primary CTA (0 10px 26px -8px rgba(99,102,241,.7)), use skeleton shimmer during extraction, and add prefers-reduced-motion + :focus-visible rings.
