SCHEME 01

Neo-Brutalism

Loud, confident, zero gradients. 4px black outlines, hard offset shadows, flat blocks of teal + action orange — the palette you liked, executed with attitude. Reads as a deliberate Gen-Z brand, not a render.

#0F766E #F97316 #FFD93D #000000
  • Hard 4–5px borders + solid offset shadows = unmistakably hand-built.
  • Archivo Black headlines, slightly rotated badges, no blur, no gradient.
  • Press states “mechanically” depress (shadow collapses) — tactile.

SCHEME 02

8-bit Pixel

Arcade nostalgia: a chunky pixel calendar with a blinking spark, Press Start 2P type, CRT scanlines and box-shadow pixel borders. Genuinely fun and obviously crafted frame-by-frame — animation is part of the brand.

FF8A3D 2DD4BF FF5DA2 FFD93D
  • Limited NES-ish palette + pixelated rendering = zero “smooth AI gradient”.
  • Blinking spark, marquee text, instant transitions — playful motion.
  • Press Start 2P / VT323 fonts read instantly as deliberate retro.

SCHEME 04

Swiss Editorial

Strict 12-column rigor, hairline rules, oversized Inter Black type, a single electric-blue accent and a visible baseline grid. Restrained and precise — the “designed by a person with taste” end of the spectrum.

#111111 #2B43FF #FAFAF7
  • Typography is the design — no decoration to “autogenerate”.
  • Mono micro-labels + index numbers feel like a real design system.
  • 0px radius, 1px rules, one accent — disciplined, not templated.

SCHEME 05

Risograph Print

Warm recycled-paper stock, two overprinted spot inks (teal + warm red) that multiply where they overlap, a slight misregistration and a grain overlay. Tactile and analog — the opposite of a clean digital render.

Teal ink Red ink Paper
  • Overprint + grain + misregistration = visibly “printed”, not generated.
  • Limited 2-ink palette is a constraint AI rarely fakes convincingly.
  • Friendly Bricolage Grotesque keeps the playful “-ino” warmth.
